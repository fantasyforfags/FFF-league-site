ARTICLE I

Purpose & Authority

1.1 The FFF League exists to deliver a structured, competitive, and entertaining fantasy football experience while preserving fairness, integrity, and maximum levels of degenerate fun.

1.2 This Constitution is the supreme law of the League and overrides any side deals, random text messages, or “jokes” that conflict with it.

TL;DR: This document is the law. By joining, you agree to play by it.

ARTICLE II

Definitions

2.1 Defines key terms like “League,” “Member,” “Commissioner,” “Season,” “Rookie,” “Gooning” (shady draft manipulation), and “Under-the-Table Trade” (real-life bribery for fantasy players).

If you’re confused about what something means, it’s probably defined here or in the PDF.

ARTICLE III

Membership

3.1–3.3 Explains who can be in the League, how you stay in good standing (pay dues, set lineups, participate), and how you can be removed for being a problem, a ghost, or a non-paying clown.

ARTICLE IV

Commissioner & Governance

4.1–4.3 The Commissioner runs the League, enforces rules, announces deadlines, and can be overruled only by a two-thirds vote. Reasonable bias is tolerated; blatant cheating is not.

ARTICLE V

Draft Administration

5.1–5.4 Covers when and where the draft happens, live vs. remote drafting, drinking expectations, breathalyzer checks, and the hard rule: NO GOONING. If shady draft behavior is detected, picks and trades can be rewound or penalized.

ARTICLE VI

Trades

6.1–6.4 Trades go through the platform or must be clearly written. Suspicious trades can be voted on or vetoed by the League, and the Commissioner can step in fast if something is obviously tanking or collusive. Under-the-table trades involving money or favors are banned and can get you kicked out.

ARTICLE VII

Payments & Financial Regulations

7.1–7.3 Sets buy-in rules, payment deadlines, prize distribution, deductions for unpaid debts, and what happens if someone never pays. Chronic non-payers can be permanently banned and clowned on forever.

ARTICLE VIII

Rookie Regulations

8.1–8.2 Rookies may get a different buy-in. If a Rookie wins the League in their first season, they only receive half the normal champion’s payout; the other half rolls into next season’s pot. They still get full credit as champion and unlimited trash-talk rights.

ARTICLE IX

Lineups & Weekly Obligations

9.1–9.2 Every Member must set a legit lineup weekly. If you ghost your team, the Commissioner can fix your lineup to keep things fair for everyone else and may hit you with penalties for repeat offenses.

ARTICLE X

Waivers & Free Agency

10.1–10.2 Covers how waivers are run (rolling, FAAB, etc.) and bans coordinated drop/pickup schemes meant to hand players to friends. Shady patterns can be investigated and reversed.

ARTICLE XI

Competitive Integrity & Last Place Punishment

11.1–11.3 No tanking, no quitting, no giving up. Last place must complete the official punishment (embarrassing but not dangerous or illegal). Refusing the punishment = automatic removal and permanent clown status.

ARTICLE XII

Disputes, Rule Changes, Amendments

12.1–12.2 Disputes go to the Commissioner first, with time limits for scoring complaints. Rule changes and amendments require at least a two-thirds majority and normally apply starting next season.

ARTICLE XIII

Certification & Acceptance

13.1–13.2 By paying, drafting, or playing, each Member accepts all terms, punishments, and humiliation in the Constitution. The signed PDF counts as the official master copy.
